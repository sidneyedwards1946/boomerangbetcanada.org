4. Recognizing Problem Gambling

Problem gambling can affect individuals in various ways. It's important to be aware of the signs. You might be experiencing a gambling problem if you:

  • Spend more money and time gambling than you can afford.
  • Neglect responsibilities at work, home, or school due to gambling.
  • Lie about your gambling habits to family or friends.
  • Feel guilty, anxious, or depressed because of your gambling.
  • Try to win back losses by gambling more.
  • Borrow money to gamble or pay off gambling debts.
  • Have arguments with family or friends about money or gambling.
  • Lose interest in hobbies or activities you once enjoyed.

If you recognize any of these signs in yourself or someone you know, we strongly encourage you to seek help.
